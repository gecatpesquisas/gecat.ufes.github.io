Research · 2025–Atual

Change of government and tax aggressiveness: what is the impact of fiscal policies on listed firms?

Examines the impact of fiscal policy changes on tax aggressiveness among listed firms, using 2000–2024 data and a quantitative Difference-in-Differences design.

  • Period: 2025–Atual
  • Call: MCTIC/CNPq Call No. 044/2024 — Universal
  • Funding agency: National Council for Scientific and Technological Development (CNPq)
  • Funding type: Research grant and scholarship
  • Status: Ongoing
Research · 2023–Atual

IFRS 15 adoption, accounting information quality, and audit quality: empirical evidence from G20 countries

Investigates whether IFRS 15 adoption affected accounting information quality and audit quality in firms from G20 countries.

  • Period: 2023–Atual
  • Call: FAPES Call No. 28/2022 — Universal
  • Funding agency: Espírito Santo Research and Innovation Support Foundation (FAPES)
  • Funding type: Research grant and scholarship
  • Status: Ongoing
Research · 2023–Atual

Audit reporting and investment decisions: an experimental study with non-institutional investors

Analyzes how non-institutional investors react to audit report content, including audit opinion, going concern risk, and key audit matters.

  • Period: 2023–Atual
  • Call: FAPES Call No. 03/2023 — Pesquisador Capixaba Fellowship (BPC)
  • Funding agency: Espírito Santo Research and Innovation Support Foundation (FAPES)
  • Funding type: Fellowship
  • Status: Ongoing
Research · 2021–Atual

The more, the better? An analysis of the informational content of independent audit reports from 2010 to 2021

Examines the association between independent audit report content, readability, earnings management, restatements, and the explanatory power of stock prices.

  • Period: 2021–Atual
  • Call: FAPES Call No. 03/2021 — Universal
  • Funding agency: Espírito Santo Research Support Foundation (FAPES)
  • Funding type: Research grant
  • Status: Ongoing

Research · 2019–2021

What comes next? Analyzing the empirical implications of financial statement restatements

Investigated the empirical implications of financial statement restatements among B3-listed firms, considering effects on prices, audit fees, auditor changes, earnings management, and dividends.

  • Period: 2019–2021
  • Call: MCTIC/CNPq Call No. 28/2018 — Universal
  • Funding agency: National Council for Scientific and Technological Development (CNPq)
  • Funding type: Research grant and scholarship
  • Status: Completed
